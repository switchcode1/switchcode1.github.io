The Kinkajou

The kinkajou , or honey bear , is related to racoons and ringtails although they can sometimes be mistaken for ferrets or monkeys.it has mainly golden fur which is covered bty some brown fur. They have nocturnal habits and they are hunted for their fur and skins. it is a mammal that is not that rare but is not often seen. They live inside of hollow tree trunks and spend most of their time in trees. They use their five inch long toung to get tough to reach fruit which is their main diet. Currently they are of least concern on the endangered list. Kinkajous are manipulative creatures who sleep in groups. They do not like being awake in the day and if you disturb them they may attack.

The Blue Morpho Butterfly

The blue morpho is one of the largest butterflies discovered. It is a large bright blue winged butterfly with a wingspan of roughly seven inches. The wings are edged with black and on the underside there are many eye spots with a dull brown colour for camouflage. When it flies the contrasting colours make it appear as if it is disappearing and appearing. They have the same physical structure as all butterflies. They have a lifespan of only 115 days. This means they have to focus on eating and reproducing. As a caterpillar it eats leaves and pea like plants. Adults have a part on their mouth (the proboscis) to suck the juice out of dead fruits. They also eat tree sap and wet mud. As an adult it can not chew so it drinks.

Epiphytes

Eiphytes live off of other plants since they primarily have no direct connection to the ground through roots or otherwise. Because of this they have found other ways to gain nutrients by harmlessly draining moisture and nutrients from other plants. Even though they seem like them they technically aren't plants (please say this is ok) they are actually epibionts which's literal translation is "live on top of".
